About the Event

Project Expo is a premier platform for students, researchers, and tech enthusiasts to demonstrate their innovative projects and prototypes. This event celebrates creativity, technical expertise, and practical problem-solving by bringing together a diverse range of computer science and engineering projects under one roof.

From hardware innovations and software applications to IoT solutions and AI implementations, Project Expo welcomes all kinds of projects that demonstrate technical excellence and creative thinking. This is your opportunity to exhibit your creation to industry experts, peers, and potential investors while receiving valuable feedback and recognition.

Submission Requirements

  • Project abstract (300 words) due by March 15, 2025
  • Working prototype or functional demo required on exhibition day
  • Project poster (A1 size) summarizing your project
  • 2-3 minute pitch video submission by April 1, 2025

Judging Criteria

Innovation & Originality (25%)

Uniqueness of the idea, creative approach, and novelty

Technical Complexity (25%)

Technical sophistication, implementation quality, and technical challenges overcome

Impact & Utility (20%)

Practical application, social/commercial value, and problem-solving ability

Presentation & Demonstration (20%)

Project explanation, demo effectiveness, and Q&A handling

Completion & Polish (10%)

Overall finish, user experience, and project completeness

Frequently Asked Questions

Can we showcase projects that are still in development?
Yes, projects in various stages of development are welcome, but you should have a working prototype or demo that demonstrates the core functionality. If your project is in early stages, focus on showcasing your concept, design, and any completed components.
What infrastructure will be provided at the exhibition?
Each team will be provided with a table (approximately 6 feet), two chairs, and access to power outlets. Wi-Fi connectivity will be available. If you have special requirements (like additional space or specific technical needs), please mention them during registration, and we'll try to accommodate.
Can the same project be submitted to multiple events?
Yes, you can submit the same project to other events like Paper Presentation or Poster Presentation, but note that each event has its specific format and evaluation criteria. You'll need to adapt your project presentation according to the requirements of each event.
Are there any restrictions on project technology or tools?
There are no specific restrictions on technologies or tools. You're encouraged to use whatever technology stack best suits your project. However, all tools and libraries used should be properly licensed, and any third-party components should be clearly acknowledged.
How long will we have to present to the judges?
Each team will get approximately 10 minutes with the judges: 5 minutes for demonstration and 5 minutes for Q&A. Additionally, you'll need to interact with visitors throughout the event as they browse through the exhibition.
