About the Event

Sherlock Homies is a cyberpunk detective challenge that tests your analytical thinking, logical reasoning, and digital forensic skills. Teams are presented with a complex cyber mystery involving data breaches, encrypted messages, and digital clues. Your mission is to solve the case by connecting the dots, uncovering hidden evidence, and identifying the culprit before time runs out.

This immersive event combines elements of escape rooms, puzzle-solving, and digital forensics with a futuristic cyberpunk twist. Participants will need to analyze network logs, decrypt messages, follow digital breadcrumbs, and interrogate AI witnesses to piece together what really happened. Can your team match wits with the mysterious cyber-criminal?

Case Format & Investigation Process

Mystery Structure

  • Teams are presented with a complex cyber-crime scenario to solve
  • The investigation unfolds in three phases: Evidence Collection, Analysis, and Final Deduction
  • Each team works from their own investigation station equipped with necessary tools
  • Multiple evidence types must be analyzed including digital files, encrypted messages, and network logs
  • Teams can request specific tools or "expert consultations" using investigation points

Investigation Skills

Digital Forensics

Examining digital footprints, metadata analysis, and recovering deleted information

Cryptography

Decoding encrypted messages, breaking simple ciphers, and password analysis

Logical Deduction

Connecting evidence, establishing timelines, and eliminating impossible scenarios

Social Engineering Analysis

Identifying manipulation tactics, analyzing suspect communications, and detecting deception

Scoring System

  • Case Solution: 60% - Correctly identifying the culprit, method, and motive
  • Evidence Found: 20% - Percentage of total evidence pieces discovered
  • Time Efficiency: 10% - Faster solutions receive more points
  • Deduction Process: 10% - Logical reasoning used to connect evidence

Frequently Asked Questions

Do we need prior cybersecurity experience?
No specialized cybersecurity knowledge is required. The puzzles and challenges are designed to be solvable through logical reasoning and critical thinking. We provide all necessary tools and instructions. However, a basic understanding of computer concepts will be helpful.
What should we bring to the event?
Just bring your student ID and your thinking caps! All investigation equipment and materials will be provided at the venue. You may bring a notebook for taking notes during your investigation, though digital note-taking options will also be available.
How difficult are the cases?
The cases are designed with multiple layers of complexity. Some clues are straightforward while others require deeper analysis. We've balanced the difficulty to ensure it's challenging but solvable within the given time frame. Teams that methodically collect and analyze evidence will be able to solve the case.
Are there any hints available during the event?
Yes, teams can request hints using their "investigation points." Each team starts with a limited number of points that can be used to unlock hints or request special investigative tools. However, using hints will deduct from your final score, so use them strategically!
Can we use the internet during the investigation?
Limited internet access will be provided, but only to specific resources relevant to the investigation. General web browsing will be restricted to prevent teams from searching for direct solutions. The case is unique to our event and all necessary information to solve it will be included in the evidence provided.
