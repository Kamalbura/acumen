About the Event

Paper Presentation provides a platform for students and researchers to present their innovative ideas, research findings, and technical solutions. It's an opportunity to showcase your academic knowledge and enhance your presentation skills while receiving valuable feedback from experts in the field.

Participants will get a chance to present their papers on various cutting-edge topics related to computer science and engineering. This event encourages critical thinking, research methodology, and effective communication of complex technical concepts.

Rules & Guidelines

Paper Submission Guidelines

  • Papers must be submitted in IEEE format with a maximum of 6 pages.
  • Submit your papers by March 25, 2025 to papers@acumenfest.in
  • Papers should be original work and not published elsewhere.
  • Plagiarism will result in immediate disqualification.
  • Papers will be reviewed by a panel of experts, and selected papers will be invited for presentation.

Presentation Guidelines

  • Each team will be given 12 minutes for presentation and 3 minutes for Q&A.
  • Presentations should be in PowerPoint or PDF format.
  • Participants must bring their own laptops. Projectors will be provided.
  • The presentation should cover the problem statement, methodology, results, and conclusion.
  • All team members must be present during the presentation.

Judging Criteria

Technical Content (40%)

Depth of research, methodology, analysis, and results

Innovation & Originality (25%)

Novelty of the idea, uniqueness, and creative approach

Presentation Skills (20%)

Clarity, organization, communication, and handling of Q&A

Visual Aids & Format (15%)

Quality of slides, adherence to format, and proper citations

Frequently Asked Questions

Can I submit a paper that I've presented elsewhere?
No, all submissions must be original work that hasn't been published or presented at other conferences or competitions.
What topics are acceptable for the paper presentation?
We accept papers on a wide range of Computer Science topics including but not limited to: Artificial Intelligence, Machine Learning, Cybersecurity, Cloud Computing, IoT, Blockchain, Big Data Analytics, and Software Engineering.
Is there a specific format for the paper submission?
Yes, papers must be submitted in IEEE format with a maximum of 6 pages. The template can be downloaded from our website. All submissions should be in PDF format.
What if I can't be physically present for the presentation?
Physical presence is mandatory for the presentation. Virtual presentations are not allowed for this competition.
Will I get a certificate even if I don't win?
Yes, all participants who present their papers will receive a certificate of participation.
