About the Event

The Jail Break Challenge is ACUMEN 2025's premier cybersecurity event where participants take on the role of ethical hackers attempting to break through a series of increasingly complex security challenges. This event tests your knowledge of network security, cryptography, reverse engineering, and web vulnerabilities in a safe and controlled environment.

Designed by cybersecurity experts, this challenge simulates real-world security scenarios that require creative thinking, technical expertise, and persistence. Whether you're a beginner curious about cybersecurity or an experienced hacker looking to test your skills, the Jail Break Challenge offers exciting puzzles for all skill levels.

Challenge Format

Structure

  • The competition consists of multiple levels of "security cells" that teams must break through.
  • Each level focuses on a different area of cybersecurity (web security, cryptography, binary exploitation, etc.).
  • Teams start with level 1 and must solve each level to unlock the next.
  • Challenges increase in difficulty as teams progress.
  • The goal is to escape all cells within the time limit to achieve the highest score.

Technical Requirements

  • Teams should bring their own laptops with Kali Linux or similar security tools.
  • Virtual machines will be provided for challenges requiring specific environments.
  • Basic knowledge of command-line interfaces, programming, and networking is essential.
  • Internet access will be provided, but with restricted connectivity to specific resources.
  • All exploits and techniques must be performed only on designated target systems.

Scoring System

Points per Level

Each level awards points based on difficulty (100-500 points)

Time Bonus

Earlier completion earns additional points (10 points per minute saved)

Hint Penalty

Using hints reduces the points earned for that level (25% reduction per hint)

Method Rating

Creative or efficient solutions may earn bonus points at judges' discretion

Frequently Asked Questions

What kind of cybersecurity skills do I need?
Basic knowledge of network concepts, web technologies, cryptography, and programming will be helpful. We have challenges for all skill levels, from beginner to advanced. The early levels are accessible to those with fundamental computer science knowledge.
Are there any tools we should install beforehand?
We recommend having Kali Linux (either installed or as a virtual machine), a text editor, Python, and common cybersecurity tools. A detailed preparation guide will be sent to registered teams two weeks before the event.
Is this event legal? Are we hacking actual systems?
Absolutely legal! All challenges are conducted in isolated environments specifically built for this competition. You'll only be attacking systems that are designed to be attacked, and all activities remain within the boundaries of the competition network.
What if we get stuck on a challenge?
Hints will be available for each level, but using them will reduce the points earned. We encourage teams to collaborate internally and try multiple approaches before requesting hints.
