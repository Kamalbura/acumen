CyberHack - 24 Hour Hackathon

Apr 10-11, 2025
10:00 AM - 10:00 AM (24 Hours)
CSE Department, Main Building
Team Size: 2-4 members

CyberHack is a 24-hour coding marathon where teams work to develop innovative solutions to real-world problems. Participants will have access to mentors, resources, and plenty of food to fuel their creativity. This year's themes focus on AI-driven solutions, cybersecurity, and sustainable technology.

Rules & Guidelines

  • Teams must consist of 2-4 members.
  • Projects must be developed entirely during the hackathon.
  • Use of open-source libraries and APIs is allowed.
  • Teams must submit their code to the designated repository by the deadline.
  • Final presentations will be 5 minutes with 3 minutes for Q&A.

Bug Busters - Code Debugging Challenge

Apr 11, 2025
2:00 PM - 5:00 PM
Lab 301, CSE Block
Individual Participation

Bug Busters is an individual coding competition focused on debugging. Participants will be given broken code snippets in various languages and will need to identify and fix the bugs within a limited time frame. This event tests both logical thinking and coding proficiency.

Rules & Guidelines

  • Individual participation only.
  • Participants may use any programming language from the provided options.
  • Internet access will be restricted during the competition.
  • Points awarded based on number of bugs fixed and time taken.
  • The judge's decision will be final.

NeuralNexus - AI Challenge

Apr 12, 2025
10:00 AM - 4:00 PM
AI Lab, CSE Department
Team Size: 1-2 members

NeuralNexus challenges participants to develop AI-powered solutions for complex problems. This year's challenge involves creating a machine learning model for predictive analysis in healthcare data. Teams will work with provided datasets and present their solution approach and results.

Rules & Guidelines

  • Teams of 1-2 members allowed.
  • Pre-trained models may be used but must be disclosed.
  • Solutions will be judged on accuracy, innovation, and explanation.
  • Datasets will be provided on registration.
  • Final presentation should not exceed 7 minutes.
